Private rented sector (PRS) exemptions register

Guidance on the exemptions register for all private rented properties that cannot comply with the 2018 ‘Minimum Level of Energy Efficiency’ standard (MEES) of meeting Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) band E.

This page applies to both domestic and non-domestic private rented properties in England and Wales. You only need to apply for an exemption if your property is covered by the Minimum Energy Efficiency Standard (MEES) Regulations. Find out more about these regulations for:

  • Domestic properties
  • Non-domestic properties

Check the exemption register for a property

You can search the register for details of exempt properties: Check the exemptions register.

Registering an exemption

If your property meets the criteria for any of the exemptions, you will be able to let it once you have registered the exemption on the PRS Exemptions Register.

If you already have an account, you can use this service to:

  • register an exemption
  • manage existing exemptions

Before you start

Before you apply, make sure you:

  • have details of the property (address and type of property)
  • understand which type of exemption you want to register
  • the evidence required for that exemption (see evidence checker or guidance)

You should be aware that:

  • you cannot amend the evidence and exemption data once you have submitted the application
  • the exemption applies immediately once you have registered
  • you cannot submit more than 10 documents as evidence - each document must be no larger than 4MB
  • documents can be in the formats pdf, png, jpg, jpeg, doc or docx
! Warning You can be fined up to £1,000 if you provide false or misleading information.

Registering a property for the first time or if you need help

If this is the first time registering a property or you're unsure about if the regulations apply

There are various exemptions that apply to the prohibition on letting a property with an energy efficiency rating below E.

Read detailed guidance on the exemptions and evidence required.

Information required for all exemptions:

  • address of the property
  • type of exemption you are registering
  • copy of a valid EPC for the property

You will also need to provide exemption specific evidence.

If you're unsure if your property needs to comply with the MEES regulations or this is your first time registering a property, we've added a few more steps to help make sure this is needed for your property.

You will be asked to create an account as a landlord or agent, if you do not already have an account.

You should be aware that:

  • you cannot amend the evidence and exemption data once you have submitted the application
  • the exemption applies immediately once you have registered
  • you cannot submit more than 10 documents as evidence - each document must be no larger than 4MB
  • documents can be in the formats pdf, png, jpg, jpeg, doc or docx

Assisted digital support to register an exemption

If you need assisted digital support to register an exemption please get in touch by email: esnz@cust-serv-details.com or call the digital helpline on 0800 098 7950.

The Assisted Digital service provides digital support in lodging an exemption on the register. The service is not able to provide advice on whether individual properties meet the criteria for an exemption..
